Our Mission



The CyREN Lab advances the cyber simulation domain and cybersecurity data collection through research, modeling, experimentation, product evaluation, and collaboration activities. We aim to engineer a simulation testbed that enables on-demand environment configuration and customization for test-specific environment controls. This R&D effort builds upon traditional simulation design and honeypot research to produce realistically camouflaged decoy simulations. Such simulations are capable of empirical cyber intelligence collection, hypothesis testing, and incident response planning.

Camouflaged Simulations

How do you build a simulation that can collect empirically valid cyber threat intelligence?

Our lab implements a wide range of camoflauge techniques, including virtualization technologies, automation, and synthetic data. These techniques allow for empirical experimentation and cyber threat intelligence collection.

Project Evaluations

Thousands of diferent intrusion detection systems exist, but how can they be objectively evaluated?

Our lab uses scientific simulations to assess and objectively measure the performance and usability of instrusion detection systems.

Cyber Best Practices

How can cyber security best practices be evaluated?

Our lab combines experimental design with rigorous research methods to develop conclusions on potential cyber best practices that are both generalizable and empirically valid.
